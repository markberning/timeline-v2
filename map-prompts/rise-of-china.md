## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL RISE OF CHINA PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in this prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are rendered in light blue. Do not render water as tan, cream, or white. Deserts are pale yellow. Land is beige/tan.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

---

The chapter title "Chapter N: <Title>" should appear at the top of the map image, centered, in a clean serif font, inside a header bar that spans the full width of the image. The header bar is the ONLY bordered element of the image. The left, right, and bottom edges of the image have NO border or frame — the map geography extends fully to all three of those edges, edge-to-edge. Do not draw a rectangular frame around the map on the left, right, or bottom sides.

**CHINA ORIENTATION RULES (Modern):**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The Yellow River is the MAIN northern river, flowing west-to-east with a large loop around the Ordos Desert. The Yangtze River is the MAIN southern river, flowing west-to-east further south. Both empty into the sea on the east coast.
3. Beijing is in the NORTH-EAST on the north China plain. Tianjin is its coastal port. Shanghai is on the coast where the Yangtze meets the East China Sea. Wuhan is on the middle Yangtze. Chongqing and Chengdu are on the upper Yangtze in the SOUTH-WEST interior (the Sichuan Basin). Xi'an is in the north-center interior.
4. The Pearl River Delta is the far SOUTH coast: Guangzhou inland, Shenzhen and Hong Kong adjacent on the east side of the delta, Zhuhai and Macau on the west side. Shantou is on the coast east of the delta; Xiamen is further up the Fujian coast, facing Taiwan.
5. Taiwan is an island off the SOUTHEAST coast across the Taiwan Strait. The South China Sea is the far southern sea, with the Spratly Islands in its south. Manchuria is the far NORTHEAST; Dalian is its main port. Xinjiang (with Urumqi) is the FAR WEST desert region; the Tibetan Plateau is the SOUTH-WEST highland.
6. The Himalayan frontier with India, including the Galwan Valley, is on the far SOUTH-WEST edge. Land routes west run from the northwest through Xinjiang toward Central Asia.
