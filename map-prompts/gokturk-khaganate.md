## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL GOKTURK PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are light blue. Deserts are pale yellow. Steppe and grassland are light green. Land is beige or tan. Mountains are gray-brown.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**STEPPE AND CENTRAL ASIA ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The Mongolian steppe is in the EAST — modern Mongolia, north of the Gobi Desert and the Great Wall of China.
3. The Orkhon Valley is in CENTRAL Mongolia, in the Khangai mountain region.
4. The Altai Mountains run NORTHWEST-to-SOUTHEAST and separate the Mongolian steppe from Central Asia and Sogdiana.
5. Sogdiana / Transoxiana is in the WEST — modern Uzbekistan, between the Oxus River (Amu Darya, southern) and the Jaxartes River (Syr Darya, northern). Bukhara and Samarkand are in Sogdiana.
6. The Tian Shan range runs WEST-to-EAST, north of the Tarim Basin and south of the Dzungarian Basin.
7. The Tarim Basin is the desert basin SOUTH of the Tian Shan, with oasis cities ringing the central Taklamakan Desert: Kashgar (west), Khotan (south), Kucha (north), Turfan (northeast), Dunhuang (east).
8. Chang'an (Tang capital) is in CENTRAL-NORTHWEST China, SOUTH of the Great Wall.
9. Constantinople is on the Bosphorus, at the FAR WEST edge of any wide-Eurasian map.
10. Ctesiphon (Sasanian capital) is in MESOPOTAMIA, on the Tigris near modern Baghdad.
