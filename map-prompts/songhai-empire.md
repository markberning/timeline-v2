4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are rendered in light blue. Do not render water as tan, cream, or white. Deserts are pale yellow. Land is beige/tan. River floodplains are soft green.

**SONGHAI EMPIRE ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right. The Atlantic Ocean is on the western (left) edge of the map. The Sahara Desert covers the upper (northern) half. The Sahel band runs east-to-west across the middle. The savanna and forest belt is at the bottom (south).
2. The Niger River bends NORTH from the Guinea highlands into the Sahel, runs EAST past Timbuktu in a great east-west arc, then turns SOUTHEAST at the Niger Bend toward modern Nigeria. The Niger does NOT run straight east-to-west; the great northern arc is the defining shape of the river.
3. Gao sits on the EASTERN side of the Niger Bend, near the start of the southeast turn. Timbuktu sits NORTHWEST of Gao on the northern side of the Niger River bend, on the southern edge of the Sahara. Djenné sits SOUTHWEST of Timbuktu in the Inner Niger Delta. Walata sits FAR WEST of Timbuktu in southern Mauritania.
4. Tondibi is just NORTH of Gao, on the Niger River about 35 miles upstream from Gao itself. Tondibi is NOT west of Timbuktu and is NOT in the Inner Niger Delta — it is in the eastern Niger Bend near Gao.
5. The Inner Niger Delta is a large flooded grassland zone in the central Niger arc, between Djenné and Timbuktu. It is NOT a coastal river delta; it is an inland seasonal flood plain.
6. Morocco / the Maghreb is at the upper LEFT (northwest), separated from the Sahel by the Sahara. Marrakesh is in southern Morocco. The Saadian invasion of 1591 came SOUTH from Marrakesh through Taghaza to Tondibi — the invasion route runs north-to-south, not east-to-west.
7. Taghaza is a salt-mining oasis deep in the central Sahara, far north of Timbuktu and roughly midway between Marrakesh and the Niger Bend. The Mossi kingdoms are SOUTH of the Niger Bend, in the upper Volta basin (modern Burkina Faso). The Hausa city-states are EAST of Gao, in modern northern Nigeria.
