**RUSSIAN EMPIRE / EURASIA ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right. This is the largest land empire on earth and the east–west extent is enormous; do not rotate or tilt the map and do not stretch the landmass into a thin strip.
2. The fixed geography, west to east: **St Petersburg** sits on the Baltic in the far northwest (founded 1703). **Moscow** is in the center-west, roughly 400 miles southeast of St Petersburg. The **Volga River** runs from near Moscow south to the **Caspian Sea**. 5. To the south-center, beyond the Caspian and the steppe, are the Russian–Central-Asian khanates: **Tashkent**, **Bukhara**, **Khiva**, **Kokand**, the Turkestan oases. Afghanistan and the borders of British India lie just beyond them.
