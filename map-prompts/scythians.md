## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL SCYTHIAN PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this. Every word on the final map must appear verbatim somewhere in this prompt.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are light blue. Deserts are pale yellow. Land is beige/tan. Grassland/steppe is light olive or muted green-tan.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**EURASIAN STEPPE ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The **Eurasian steppe** is a belt of grassland stretching roughly 5,000 miles (8,000 km) from HUNGARY (far west) to MONGOLIA (far east). It runs roughly EAST-WEST across the map.
3. The **Black Sea** is in the SOUTHWEST of the Scythian heartland. The **Caspian Sea** is EAST of the Black Sea, roughly 600 miles to the east. The **Aral Sea** is further east still.
4. The **Pontic steppe** (the Scythian heartland) is the grassland NORTH of the Black Sea, in modern UKRAINE and SOUTHERN RUSSIA. It stretches from the **Danube River** in the west to the **Don River** in the east.
5. The **Dnieper River** (dnee-EH-per) flows SOUTH through the center of the Pontic steppe into the Black Sea. It is the central river of the Scythian heartland.
6. The **Caucasus Mountains** run EAST-WEST between the Black Sea and the Caspian Sea, forming a barrier between the steppe (to the north) and the Near East (to the south).
7. The **Ural Mountains** run roughly NORTH-SOUTH through western Russia, dividing the Pontic/western steppe from the Central Asian/eastern steppe. They are low and broken — not a serious barrier.
8. The **Altai Mountains** are in the FAR EAST of the Scythian cultural zone, on the borders of modern Russia, China, Mongolia, and Kazakhstan — roughly 3,000 miles east of the Pontic steppe.
9. The **Zagros Mountains** are in WESTERN IRAN, separating Mesopotamia from the Iranian plateau. They are SOUTH of the Caucasus, across the Near East.
