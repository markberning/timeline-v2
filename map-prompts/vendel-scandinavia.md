## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL VENDEL SCANDINAVIA PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are light blue. Land is beige/tan. Mountains are light brown.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**SCANDINAVIAN ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The core of this world is the **Mälaren region** of east-central Sweden: Uppland (the area around Lake Mälaren), with Vendel, Valsgärde, Old Uppsala, and Helgö all within roughly 50 miles of each other, north and northwest of modern Stockholm.
3. **Gotland** is a large Baltic island sitting to the SOUTHEAST of the Swedish mainland, roughly midway across the Baltic. **Öland** is a narrower island just off the southeast coast of Sweden, south of Gotland.
4. **Denmark** and the Jutland peninsula are to the SOUTHWEST of Sweden. The island of **Zealand** (with Lejre) is off the east coast of Jutland, separated from Sweden by the narrow strait of the Kattegat. **Ribe** is on the southwest coast of Jutland, facing the North Sea.
5. The **Danevirke** runs across the narrow waist of the Jutland peninsula at its southern base, facing the continent to the SOUTH.
6. **Norway's** fjord coast runs WEST and NORTHWEST of the Swedish interior, along the Scandinavian mountain spine.
7. The **Baltic Sea** fills the map center-east, stretching east toward the Gulf of Finland. The **Kattegat** is the strait between Denmark and Sweden. The **North Sea** is to the WEST, beyond Jutland and the Norwegian coast.
8. **Frisia** and **Dorestad** are far to the SOUTHWEST, across the North Sea, on the coast of the modern Netherlands.
9. **Grobiņa** is on the EAST Baltic coast, in what is now Latvia — far to the east across the Baltic from the Swedish mainland.
10. **Saaremaa** (with the Salme site) is an Estonian island in the EASTERN Baltic, north of the Latvian coast.
11. **Lindisfarne** is a small island off the northeast coast of Britain, far to the SOUTHWEST across the North Sea from the Scandinavian heartland.
12. Key waters: Baltic Sea, North Sea, Lake Mälaren, Kattegat.
