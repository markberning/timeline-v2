**ASUKA-NARA JAPAN ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The Japanese archipelago runs roughly northeast-to-southwest. Honshu is the largest island, Kyushu is at the southwest, Shikoku sits in the inland sea between them, and Hokkaido is at the far northeast.
3. The Korean Peninsula is to the WEST across the Korea Strait, with Baekje in the southwest, Silla in the southeast, and Goguryeo in the north.
4. The Asuka basin and Nara are in the YAMATO region of central Honshu, in the wide plain south of modern Kyoto. Asuka is at the southern edge of the Yamato basin; Nara (Heijo-kyo) is just to the north; Heian-kyo (modern Kyoto) is further north still.
5. Naniwa (modern Osaka) is on the coast just west of the Yamato basin, on Osaka Bay where the Yodo River meets the Inland Sea.
6. Otsu and Lake Biwa are northeast of the Yamato basin, in the Omi region.
7. Dazaifu and Mizuki are in northern Kyushu, near modern Fukuoka, the administrative center facing the Korean peninsula.
8. The Yamato Sea (Sea of Japan) is to the WEST of Honshu; the Pacific Ocean is to the EAST.
