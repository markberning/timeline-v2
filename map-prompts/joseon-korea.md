**JOSEON KOREA ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The Korean Peninsula is a roughly rectangular north-south landmass jutting south from the Asian continent, with the Yellow Sea on the WEST, the East Sea (Sea of Japan) on the EAST, and the Korea Strait at the SOUTH separating Korea from the Japanese island of Kyushu.
3. The Yalu River and Tumen River form the NORTHERN border of the peninsula, separating Korea from Manchuria. The Yalu flows southwest into the Yellow Sea; the Tumen flows northeast into the East Sea.
4. Hanyang (Seoul) is in the CENTER-WEST of the peninsula on the Han River, roughly halfway between the northern and southern coasts. Pyongyang is in the NORTHWEST on the Taedong River. Busan is at the SOUTHEAST tip on the Korea Strait. Kaesong is just NORTH of Hanyang. Suwon is just SOUTH of Hanyang. Gyeongju is in the SOUTHEAST.
5. Ganghwa Island is just WEST of Hanyang at the mouth of the Han River where it meets the Yellow Sea. Jeju Island is the large island roughly 60 miles SOUTH of the southern Korean coast.
6. Manchuria is to the NORTHWEST and NORTH (across the Yalu); the Russian Maritime Province is to the NORTHEAST (across the Tumen). Japan is to the EAST (across the East Sea) and SOUTHEAST (Kyushu, across the Korea Strait). China is to the WEST (across the Yellow Sea).
