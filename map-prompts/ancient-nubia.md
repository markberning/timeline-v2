## Chapter 1

Create a simple, clean map for Chapter 1 of a mobile reading app about Ancient Nubia. The chapter is called "Land of the Bow" and covers the geographic setting of Nubia and the A-Group culture along the Nile south of Egypt between about 3800 and 2800 BCE, including the famous Qustul Cemetery L and the "were they kings?" archaeological controversy.

**Map requirements:**
- Show the Nile valley from Memphis (just south of modern Cairo) down to Khartoum (where the White and Blue Nile meet), with the surrounding desert and the Red Sea coast
- Mark all six cataracts of the Nile, numbered 1 through 6 from north to south, as small labeled bars across the river
- Muted, warm color palette (beige/tan land, soft green for the Nile flood strip, light blue water, pale yellow desert)
- NO sidebars, NO bottom panels, NO timelines, NO artifact illustrations, NO infographic elements
- Just the map with site markers and minimal labels

**Cities/sites to mark (small dots with labels):**
- Memphis (north, near modern Cairo — Old Kingdom Egyptian capital)
- Aswan / First Cataract (Egypt's southern frontier town)
- Qustul (in Lower Nubia, just south of Aswan — site of the contested A-Group royal cemetery)
- Kerma (in Upper Nubia, just south of the Third Cataract — to be introduced in Chapter 3)
- Khartoum (at the far south, where the White and Blue Nile meet)

**Region labels (subtle, gray text):**
- Egypt
- Lower Nubia (between the First and Second Cataracts — "Wawat")
- Upper Nubia (between the Second and Sixth Cataracts — "Yam / Kush")
- Eastern Desert
- Western Desert
- Red Sea
- White Nile
- Blue Nile

**One annotation:**
A shaded band running from just south of Aswan down to roughly the Second Cataract, labeled "A-Group Culture Heartland, ~3800–2800 BCE"
