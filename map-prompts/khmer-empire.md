**KHMER ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The Tonlé Sap is a large lake in central Cambodia, elongated northwest-to-southeast. Angkor sits just NORTH of the lake's northwestern end. Phnom Penh sits at the lake's southeastern end, at the river confluence.
3. The Mekong River enters from the NORTH (out of Laos), flows SOUTH through eastern Cambodia past Phnom Penh, then fans into the Mekong Delta in southern Vietnam and empties into the South China Sea at the SOUTHEAST.
4. The Tonlé Sap River is the short channel linking the Tonlé Sap lake (northwest) to the Mekong at Phnom Penh (southeast). It is the river that reverses flow seasonally.
5. Champa is a coastal strip along the central Vietnam coast, EAST of Cambodia, facing the South China Sea. Dai Viet is the Red River area of northern Vietnam, at the far NORTHEAST.
6. The Dangrek Mountains are an east-west escarpment forming Cambodia's NORTHERN edge, separating the Cambodian plain (south) from the Khorat Plateau of northeast Thailand (north). Preah Vihear sits on the Dangrek.
7. Lopburi (Lavo), Sukhothai, and Ayutthaya are to the WEST and NORTHWEST, in the Chao Phraya River basin of central Thailand. The Gulf of Thailand is to the SOUTHWEST.
8. Phnom Kulen is a plateau about 30 miles NORTHEAST of Angkor. Koh Ker is about 75 miles northeast of Angkor. Roluos (Hariharalaya) is just SOUTHEAST of Angkor. Oc Eo is in the Mekong Delta in the far south.
