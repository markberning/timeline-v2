## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL ZAPOTEC CIVILIZATION PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, and rivers are light blue. Land is beige/tan. Mountain terrain is light brown.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**OAXACA ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The **Valley of Oaxaca** is shaped like an upside-down Y, with three arms converging at a central point. The **Etla arm** runs to the NORTH. The **Tlacolula arm** runs to the EAST. The **Zimatlán arm** runs to the SOUTH.
3. **Monte Albán** sits at the CENTRAL JUNCTION of the three arms, on a mountain ridge 1,300 feet above the valley floor.
4. **San José Mogote** is in the NORTHERN (Etla) arm of the valley.
5. The **Mixteca Alta** (Mixtec highlands) is to the NORTHWEST.
6. The **Cuicatlán Cañada** is a narrow canyon corridor to the NORTH, connecting the valley to the central Mexican highlands.
7. The **Isthmus of Tehuantepec** is to the SOUTHEAST, the narrowest point in Mexico.
8. **Teotihuacan** is ~250 miles (400 km) to the NORTHWEST in the Basin of Mexico.
