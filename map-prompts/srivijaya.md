## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL SRIVIJAYA PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in this prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are rendered in light blue. Do not render water as tan, cream, or white. Land is beige/tan.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**SRIVIJAYA ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. Sumatra is the long northwest-to-southeast island in the center-west of the map. The Strait of Malacca runs along its NORTHEAST coast, between Sumatra and the Malay Peninsula.
3. The Malay Peninsula extends SOUTH from mainland Southeast Asia (modern Thailand and Myanmar to the north), with its southern tip near the modern city of Singapore.
4. Java is the long east-west island SOUTH of Sumatra, separated by the narrow Sunda Strait. Borneo is the large island EAST of Sumatra and northeast of Java.
5. India is at the WEST edge of the map. Southern China and the Tang/Song ports of Guangzhou and Quanzhou are at the NORTHEAST edge. The Bay of Bengal lies between India and Sumatra; the South China Sea lies between mainland Southeast Asia and Borneo/Philippines.
6. Palembang is on the SOUTHEASTERN coast of Sumatra, on the Musi River. Jambi is north of Palembang, on the Batang Hari River. Kedah is on the WESTERN coast of the Malay Peninsula.
7. Borobudur is in central Java, in the modern Yogyakarta region. The Moluccas (Spice Islands) are far to the EAST, beyond Borneo and Sulawesi.
