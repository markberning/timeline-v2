# Map Prompts — The Industrial Revolution (20 chapters)

See [README.md](README.md) for house style. Save output to `public/maps/industrial-revolution/chapter-{N}.png`, then run the WebP optimizer on the new maps only.

Chapters in this file: **Ch 1–20**. Most maps are Great Britain and Ireland; Ch 15 widens to western and central Europe; Ch 16 widens to the eastern United States and the Atlantic; Ch 18, 19, and 20 widen to a world frame.

---

## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL INDUSTRIAL REVOLUTION PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in this prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are rendered in light blue. Do not render water as tan, cream, or white. Land is beige/tan; coalfields are shaded dark grey; industrial cities are marked with a small gear or chimney glyph.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

---

The chapter title "Chapter N: <Title>" should appear at the top of the map image, centered, in a clean serif font, inside a header bar that spans the full width of the image. The header bar is the ONLY bordered element of the image. The left, right, and bottom edges of the image have NO border or frame — the map geography extends fully to all three of those edges, edge-to-edge. Do not draw a rectangular frame around the map on the left, right, or bottom sides.

**BRITAIN ORIENTATION RULES (apply to every chapter unless the chapter says otherwise):**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right. The Atlantic Ocean and the Irish Sea are on the west (left); the North Sea is on the east (right); the English Channel is at the bottom, separating England from France.
2. Great Britain is one large island. Scotland is the north; England is the centre and south; Wales is the western bulge of the south half. Ireland is the separate island to the west, across the Irish Sea.
3. London is in the southeast of England on the River Thames. Bristol is in the southwest on the River Severn estuary. Liverpool and Manchester are in the northwest of England (Lancashire); Manchester is inland, Liverpool is on the coast at the Mersey.
4. Birmingham and the Black Country are in the West Midlands, in the centre of England. Coalbrookdale and the Severn Gorge are just west of Birmingham, in the Shropshire hills. The Pennine hills run north–south up the spine of northern England, between Lancashire (west) and Yorkshire (east, with Leeds).
5. Newcastle and the River Tyne are in the far northeast of England (the great northern coalfield). Stockton and Darlington are just south of Newcastle. Glasgow and New Lanark are in southern Scotland; Edinburgh is east of Glasgow.
6. The major coalfields — South Wales, the Black Country, Lancashire, Yorkshire, the North East around Newcastle, and central Scotland — are shaded dark grey beneath the towns that sit on them.
