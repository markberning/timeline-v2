**EDO JAPAN ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The Japanese archipelago runs in a long arc from northeast (Hokkaido) to southwest (Kyushu). Honshu is the largest, longest island, in the middle of the arc.
3. Kyushu is the southwestern island. Shikoku is south of western Honshu, in the Inland Sea. Hokkaido (called Ezo in Edo times) is the northern island.
4. Edo (modern Tokyo) is on the Pacific coast in the eastern half of Honshu, on Edo Bay.
5. Kyoto and Osaka are in central Honshu (the Kinai region), in the western half. Kyoto is inland; Osaka is on the Inland Sea coast.
6. Nagasaki is on the western side of northern Kyushu. Shimabara is on the southwestern Kyushu coast, west of Nagasaki.
7. The Sea of Japan is on the western side of the archipelago; the Pacific Ocean is on the eastern side.
8. Korea is to the west across the Korea Strait, separated from Kyushu by the islands of Tsushima and Iki.
