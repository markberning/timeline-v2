2. Italy is a long thin peninsula extending southeast from the Alps in the north to the toe of the boot in the south. The Apennine Mountains run down the spine of the peninsula.
3. The Adriatic Sea is on the EAST side of Italy. The Tyrrhenian Sea is on the WEST side. The Mediterranean is to the SOUTH.
4. Florence is in central-north Italy, in the Tuscan hills, on the Arno River. Rome is in central Italy, on the Tiber. Venice is in the northeastern lagoon at the head of the Adriatic. Milan is in the north, in the Po Valley. Naples is in the south, on the Bay of Naples.
5. The Po River runs west-to-east across northern Italy, through the Po Valley, emptying into the Adriatic. The Tiber runs through Rome to the Tyrrhenian Sea. The Arno runs through Florence to the Tyrrhenian Sea.
6. Sicily is the large triangular island off the toe of the Italian boot. Sardinia is the large island west of central Italy. Corsica is the large island just north of Sardinia (technically French but geographically Italian).
7. The Alps form the northern boundary of Italy with France, Switzerland, and Austria.
