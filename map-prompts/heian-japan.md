## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL HEIAN JAPAN PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in this prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are rendered in light blue. Do not render water as tan, cream, or white. Mountainous land is beige/tan with darker shading. Lowland plains are soft green.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**HEIAN JAPAN ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The Japanese archipelago consists of four main islands. From north to south: Hokkaido (far north), Honshu (the long main island), Shikoku (smaller, south of central Honshu), Kyushu (south-southwest of Honshu). Most Heian-period maps focus on Honshu and the surrounding waters.
3. The Sea of Japan is to the WEST of Honshu. The Pacific Ocean is to the EAST of Honshu. The Inland Sea (Seto Inland Sea) is the protected waterway between Honshu, Shikoku, and Kyushu, accessed from the east through Osaka Bay and from the west through the Shimonoseki Strait.
4. Kyoto (Heian-kyo) is in the south-central Honshu in the Yamashiro basin, ringed by mountains. Nara is about 25 miles south of Kyoto. Osaka is on the coast about 25 miles southwest of Kyoto, on Osaka Bay.
5. Mount Hiei is a single peak immediately northeast of Kyoto. Mount Koya is in the Kii Peninsula south of the capital, well south of Nara.
6. The Kanto plain (where modern Tokyo sits) is in eastern Honshu, with Kamakura on the coast at the southern edge of the Kanto plain.
7. The Inland Sea has many islands. Yashima is a small island off the northeast coast of Shikoku. The Shimonoseki Strait is the narrow waterway at the western tip of Honshu, between Honshu and Kyushu. Dannoura is in the Shimonoseki Strait.
