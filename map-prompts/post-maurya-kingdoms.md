## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL POST-MAURYA KINGDOMS PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, and rivers are light blue. Deserts are pale yellow. Land is beige/tan. Mountains are light brown.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**INDIA ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The **Hindu Kush** and **Karakoram** mountain ranges are in the FAR NORTHWEST. The **Ganges plain** stretches across NORTH-CENTRAL India from west to east.
3. **Gandhara** (Peshawar region) is in the NORTHWEST. **Mathura** is in NORTH-CENTRAL India. **Pataliputra** (Patna) is in the NORTHEAST of the Gangetic plain.
4. The **Deccan** plateau is SOUTH of the Vindhya Mountains. The **Western Ghats** run along the WESTERN coast. The **Eastern Ghats** run along the EASTERN coast.
5. The **Arabian Sea** is to the WEST. The **Bay of Bengal** is to the EAST. **Sri Lanka** is at the SOUTHERN tip.
6. **Bactria** (modern northern Afghanistan) is FAR NORTHWEST, across the Hindu Kush from Gandhara.
