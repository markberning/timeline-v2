## Chapter 1 — REDO (lean)

Create a simple, clean map for Chapter 1 of a mobile reading app about Ancient Japan. The chapter covers the Jōmon period and the world's oldest pottery.

The chapter title "Chapter 1: The World's Oldest Pots" should appear at the top of the map image, centered, in a clean serif font, inside a header bar that spans the full width of the image. The header bar is the ONLY bordered element of the image. The left, right, and bottom edges of the image have NO border or frame — the map geography extends fully to all three of those edges, edge-to-edge. Do not draw a rectangular frame around the map on the left, right, or bottom sides.

**CRITICAL RULES:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.**
2. **Spell every label exactly as written below. Do not invent words. The word "south" is spelled `s-o-u-t-h` — never write `somof` or any other variant.**
3. **Site labels are name-only — no parentheticals on the dots.**
4. **North is at the top. East is to the right.**
5. **All water is light blue.**
6. **Do not invent any labels, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**Map requirements:**
- Full Japanese archipelago: Hokkaido, Honshu, Shikoku, Kyushu
- Muted warm palette

**Sites — 4 dots, name-only labels:**
- **Sannai-Maruyama** — northern Honshu (modern Aomori)
- **Itoigawa** — Sea of Japan coast, central Honshu
- **Ōyu** — northern Honshu
- **Komakino** — northern Honshu

**Region labels (gray, 4 max):**
- Hokkaido
- Honshu
- Sea of Japan
- Pacific Ocean

**One annotation:**
- A shaded zone across central and northern Honshu with this caption: "Jōmon heartland, ~14,000–300 BCE — hunter-gatherer-fishers, the world's oldest pottery, long-distance obsidian and jade trade by sea."

**Style:** Landscape orientation, simple and elegant.
