## Chapter 3 — REDO (lean)

Create a simple, clean map for Chapter 3 of a mobile reading app about the Kingdom of Aksum. The chapter covers the Aksum stelae field, the Great Stela's collapse, and the start of Aksumite gold coinage.

The chapter title "Chapter 3: Stone and Gold" should appear at the top of the map image, centered, in a clean serif font, inside a header bar that spans the full width of the image. The header bar is the ONLY bordered element of the image. The left, right, and bottom edges of the image have NO border or frame — the map geography extends fully to all three of those edges, edge-to-edge. Do not draw a rectangular frame around the map on the left, right, or bottom sides.

**CRITICAL RULES:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.**
2. **Spell every label exactly as written below. Do not invent words.**
3. **Site labels are name-only — no parentheticals on the dots.**
4. **North is at the top. East is to the right.**
5. **All water is light blue.**
6. **Do not invent any labels, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**Map requirements:**
- Focused view of the Aksumite heartland: Ethiopian highlands, the Red Sea coast, with Yemen visible across the water
- Muted warm palette

**Sites — 3 dots, name-only labels:**
- **Aksum** — Ethiopian highlands
- **Gobedra Hill** — ~2.5 miles west of Aksum
- **Adulis** — Red Sea coast east of Aksum

**Region labels (gray, 4 max):**
- Ethiopian Highlands
- Red Sea
- Afar Depression
- Arabian Peninsula

**One annotation:**
- A highlighted zone at Aksum with this caption: "Stelae field — Great Stela, 108 ft and 520 tons, fell during erection ~300 CE. The largest monolith humans ever attempted to raise. First Aksumite gold coins struck under King Endubis ~270 CE."

**Style:** Landscape orientation, simple and elegant.

## Chapter 5 — REDO (lean)

Create a simple, clean map for Chapter 5 of a mobile reading app about the Kingdom of Aksum. The chapter covers King Kaleb's expedition across the Red Sea to Yemen in 525 CE.

The chapter title "Chapter 5: The King Who Crossed the Sea" should appear at the top of the map image, centered, in a clean serif font, inside a header bar that spans the full width of the image. The header bar is the ONLY bordered element of the image. The left, right, and bottom edges of the image have NO border or frame — the map geography extends fully to all three of those edges, edge-to-edge. Do not draw a rectangular frame around the map on the left, right, or bottom sides.

**CRITICAL RULES:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.**
2. **Spell every label exactly as written below. Do not invent words. Do not truncate phrases — never write fragments like "march t on".**
3. **Site labels are name-only — no parentheticals on the dots.**
4. **North is at the top. East is to the right.**
5. **All water is light blue.**
6. **Do not invent any labels, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**Map requirements:**
- View showing the Horn of Africa, the Red Sea, and the southern Arabian Peninsula (Yemen)
- Muted warm palette

**Sites — 4 dots, name-only labels:**
- **Aksum** — Ethiopian highlands
- **Adulis** — Red Sea coast east of Aksum (fleet assembly point)
- **Najran** — Arabian Peninsula, southwestern Saudi Arabia near the Yemen border
- **Sanaa** — central Yemen

**Region labels (gray, 4 max):**
- Ethiopian Highlands
- Red Sea
- Himyarite Kingdom
- Bab el-Mandeb

**One annotation:**
- A bold arrow from Adulis across the Red Sea to Yemen with this caption: "Kaleb's invasion, 525 CE — Aksumite fleet crosses the Bab el-Mandeb, conquers Yemen. Sasanian Persians retake Yemen in 570 CE; Aksum's overseas power ends."

**Style:** Landscape orientation, simple and elegant.
