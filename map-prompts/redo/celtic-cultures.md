## Chapter 3 — REDO (lean)

Create a simple, clean map for Chapter 3 of a mobile reading app about Celtic Cultures. The chapter covers the Celtic expansion ~400–270 BCE — to Ireland, the Po Valley, the Balkans, and Anatolia (Galatia).

The chapter title "Chapter 3: The Great Expansion" should appear at the top of the map image, centered, in a clean serif font, inside a header bar that spans the full width of the image. The header bar is the ONLY bordered element of the image. The left, right, and bottom edges of the image have NO border or frame — the map geography extends fully to all three of those edges, edge-to-edge. Do not draw a rectangular frame around the map on the left, right, or bottom sides.

**CRITICAL RULES:**

1. **Quoted sentences are reproduced verbatim with no missing words.** The Alexander quote MUST contain the word "fear" — read: "We fear nothing, except that the sky might fall on our heads".
2. **Draw each label exactly once.**
3. **Spell every label exactly as written below.**
4. **Site labels are name-only — no parentheticals on the dots.**
5. **North is at the top. East is to the right.**
6. **All water is light blue.**
7. **Do not invent any labels, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**Map requirements:**
- Wide European view from Ireland in the northwest to central Turkey in the east
- Muted warm palette

**Sites — 2 dots, name-only labels:**
- **Mediolanum** — Po Valley (modern Milan)
- **Galatia** — central Anatolia

**Region labels (gray, 5 max):**
- Celtic heartland
- Gaul
- British Isles
- Cisalpine Gaul
- Mediterranean Sea

**One annotation:**
- Bold arrows radiating from the Celtic heartland (north to the British Isles, south to the Po Valley, east to the Balkans and Anatolia) with this single caption: "Celtic expansion ~400–270 BCE — from Ireland to Turkey, the largest cultural zone in Europe. Alexander meets Celtic ambassadors, 335 BCE: 'We fear nothing, except that the sky might fall on our heads.'"

**Style:** Landscape orientation, simple and elegant.
