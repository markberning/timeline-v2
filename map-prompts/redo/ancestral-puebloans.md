## Chapter 8 — REDO (lean)

Create a simple, clean map for Chapter 8 of a mobile reading app about the Ancestral Puebloans. The chapter covers the migration destinations after the Four Corners were abandoned: the living Pueblo communities at Hopi, Zuni, Acoma, and the Rio Grande pueblos.

The chapter title "Chapter 8: Where They Went" should appear at the top of the map image, centered, in a clean serif font, inside a header bar that spans the full width of the image. The header bar is the ONLY bordered element of the image. The left, right, and bottom edges of the image have NO border or frame — the map geography extends fully to all three of those edges, edge-to-edge. Do not draw a rectangular frame around the map on the left, right, or bottom sides.

**CRITICAL RULES:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** No site, region, or annotation appears twice.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written below. Do not invent words. Do not generate any word that is not in this prompt.**
3. **Site labels are name-only — no parentheticals, no extra description on the dots.**
4. **North is at the top. East is to the right.**
5. **All water is light blue.**
6. **Do not invent any labels, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**Map requirements:**
- Wide view of the American Southwest: Four Corners at the upper-left, Rio Grande valley at the right, Hopi Mesas at left
- Dashed migration arrows from the Four Corners to the destination pueblos
- Muted warm palette

**Sites — 5 dots, name-only labels:**

- **Oraibi** — Hopi Third Mesa, northeastern Arizona
- **Zuni** — Arizona–New Mexico border

**Region labels (gray, 4 max):**

- Hopi Mesas

**One annotation:**
