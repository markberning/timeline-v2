The Japanese archipelago consists of four main islands arranged from northeast to southwest: Hokkaido (the large northern island), Honshu (the long curving central main island), Shikoku (the small island just south of Honshu across an inland sea), and Kyushu (the southwestern island closest to Korea).
3. The Sea of Japan is to the WEST of Honshu (between Japan and the Korean peninsula). The Pacific Ocean is to the EAST of the islands.

5. Aomori Prefecture is at the far northern end of Honshu, just south of the Tsugaru Strait that separates Honshu from Hokkaido.
6. Tokyo (modern) sits on the Pacific coast of central Honshu, roughly midway down the island. The Kinai region (modern Osaka–Nara–Kyoto) is in central-western Honshu, on the Inland Sea.
7. Northern Kyushu (modern Fukuoka and Saga) faces the Korea Strait at the southwestern end of the archipelago. The Inland Sea (Seto Naikai) lies between western Honshu and Shikoku.
8. The Izu Islands are a chain of small volcanic islands extending south from the Tokyo area into the Pacific.
