## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL MAYA PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this. Every word on the final map must appear verbatim somewhere in this prompt.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, and rivers are light blue. Deserts are pale yellow. Land is beige/tan. Tropical lowlands are soft green.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**MESOAMERICA / MAYA ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The **Gulf of Mexico** is to the NORTH and NORTHEAST. The **Pacific Ocean** is to the SOUTH and SOUTHWEST. The **Caribbean Sea** is to the EAST. Do not swap these.
3. The **Yucatan Peninsula** is the large peninsula projecting NORTHWARD into the Gulf of Mexico / Caribbean. It is FLAT and DRY, with thinner soil and more cenotes than the southern lowlands.
4. The **Peten** lowlands are in NORTHERN GUATEMALA and SOUTHERN MEXICO (Campeche), SOUTH of the Yucatan Peninsula proper. This is the DENSE JUNGLE heartland where most Classic Maya cities are located. Tikal, Calakmul, El Mirador, and Nakbe are all in the Peten.
5. **Tikal** is in the CENTRAL Peten. **Calakmul** is roughly 60 miles (100 km) NORTH-NORTHWEST of Tikal, in the southern Campeche region of Mexico.
6. **Palenque** is in the WESTERN lowlands, in the foothills of Chiapas, WEST of Tikal by roughly 200 miles.
7. **Copan** is in the FAR SOUTHEAST of the Maya world, in western Honduras, roughly 250 miles SOUTHEAST of Tikal.
8. The **Maya Highlands** are in SOUTHERN GUATEMALA and western Honduras — cool, volcanic, rich in obsidian and jade. They are SOUTH of the Peten lowlands.
9. The **Motagua Valley** (the jade source) is in EASTERN GUATEMALA, running east toward the Caribbean coast.
10. The **Usumacinta River** flows roughly NORTH from the highlands through the western lowlands toward the Gulf of Mexico. Yaxchilan sits on this river.
