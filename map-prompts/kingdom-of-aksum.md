## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL AKSUM PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this. Every word on the final map must appear verbatim somewhere in this prompt.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are light blue. Deserts are pale yellow. Land is beige/tan. Highland areas are soft green.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**AKSUM ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. **Aksum** is in the Ethiopian highlands, in what is now northern Ethiopia, at roughly 7,000 feet (2,100 m) elevation. It sits roughly 60 miles (100 km) WEST of the Red Sea coast.
3. **Adulis** is the Red Sea port city, on the coast of modern Eritrea near Massawa, roughly 60 miles (100 km) EAST and DOWNHILL from Aksum. Adulis is ALWAYS on the coast, east of Aksum.
4. The **Red Sea** runs roughly NNW-to-SSE between Africa and Arabia. It is EAST of the Ethiopian highlands.
5. The **Ethiopian highlands** (the plateau) occupy the left/center of the map. They descend sharply EASTWARD to the Red Sea coast (the eastern escarpment). They descend more gradually WESTWARD and NORTHWESTWARD toward the Sudanese lowlands and the Nile valley.
6. **Meroe** is in Sudan, far to the NORTHWEST of Aksum, on the Nile between the Fifth and Sixth Cataracts. Meroe is NOT near the Red Sea.
7. The **Blue Nile** originates at Lake Tana in the Ethiopian highlands and flows NORTHWEST into Sudan, where it joins the White Nile at Khartoum.
8. **Yemen** and the Arabian Peninsula are EAST of the Red Sea, across the water from the Ethiopian highlands and Adulis. The **Bab el-Mandeb** strait is the narrow crossing at the southern end of the Red Sea, about 20 miles (32 km) wide.
9. The **Afar Depression** is the low-lying desert between the eastern escarpment of the highlands and the Red Sea coast.
