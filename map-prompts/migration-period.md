## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL MIGRATION PERIOD PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are rendered in light blue. Do not render water as tan, cream, or white. Land is beige/tan. Mountains are light brown.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**MIGRATION-PERIOD ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The **Huns** come from the EAST, across the Pontic steppe north of the Black Sea. Their pressure runs west into the Germanic world.
3. The **Rhine** is the western Roman frontier, running roughly north-south through what is now western Germany, emptying into the North Sea. The **Danube** is the southern frontier, running southeast from the upper Rhine through the Balkans to the Black Sea.
4. The **406 Rhine crossing** occurred on the middle Rhine, near Mainz and Worms in what is now western Germany.
5. The Vandals, Suebi, and Alans crossed the Rhine (middle Rhine, 406), moved WEST across Gaul (modern France), then SOUTHWEST across the Pyrenees into Hispania (modern Spain and Portugal), then SOUTH across the Strait of Gibraltar into North Africa. **Carthage** is in modern Tunisia, on the southern Mediterranean coast, directly south across the Mediterranean from Sicily.
6. **Attila's Catalaunian Plains** are in northeastern Gaul, in the Champagne region of what is now northeastern France.
7. **Italy** is the central Mediterranean peninsula, running northwest-to-southeast. **Rome** is in central Italy; **Ravenna** is on the northeastern Adriatic coast; **Aquileia** is at the northeastern tip of the peninsula near the head of the Adriatic.
8. The **Lombards** entered Italy from the NORTHEAST, coming from Pannonia (modern western Hungary) through the head of the Adriatic.
9. **Constantinople** is far to the SOUTHEAST, on the Bosporus strait between the Black Sea and the Aegean.
10. Key waters: **Rhine River** (western frontier), **Danube River** (southern frontier), **Mediterranean Sea** (central), **Adriatic Sea** (separating Italy from the Balkans), **Black Sea** (northeast of the Mediterranean), **Pontic steppe** (north of the Black Sea).
