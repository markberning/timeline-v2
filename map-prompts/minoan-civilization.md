## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL MINOAN PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this. Every word on the final map must appear verbatim somewhere in this prompt.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, and rivers are light blue. Deserts are pale yellow. Land is beige/tan.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**MINOAN / AEGEAN ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. The Greek mainland (Peloponnese) is to the NORTH. Anatolia (Turkey) is to the EAST. Egypt is far to the SOUTH (show as a label only on wider maps). Libya is directly south of Crete.
2. **Crete** is the large island in the SOUTHERN Aegean Sea, oriented roughly east-west, about 160 miles (260 km) long and only 37 miles (60 km) wide. It is approximately 100 miles (160 km) south of the Greek mainland.
3. The **Cyclades** are a ring of small islands in the CENTRAL Aegean, between Crete and the Greek mainland. Thera (Santorini) is the southernmost major Cycladic island, about 70 miles (110 km) north of Crete.
4. **Knossos** is on the NORTH coast of Crete, roughly in the center of the island.
5. **Phaistos** is in the SOUTH-CENTRAL area of Crete, on the Mesara plain. It is NOT on the north coast.
6. **Malia** is EAST of Knossos, also on the north coast.
7. **Zakros** is on the FAR EASTERN tip of Crete.
8. Mount Ida (Psiloritis) is in the CENTER of Crete, the highest peak on the island.
