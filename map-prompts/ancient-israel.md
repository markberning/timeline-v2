## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL ANCIENT ISRAEL PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in this prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this. Every word on the final map must appear verbatim somewhere in this prompt.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are rendered in light blue. Do not render water as tan, cream, or white. Deserts are pale yellow. Land is beige/tan. River floodplains are soft green.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.** If it is not in the prompt, do not draw it.

**LEVANT ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. The **Mediterranean Sea** is to the west (left side of the map). The coastline runs roughly north-south along the left portion of the map.
2. The **Dead Sea** is a landlocked lake in the center-right area of the map, roughly south of the Sea of Galilee. The **Jordan River** flows north-to-south from the Sea of Galilee down into the Dead Sea.
3. The **Sea of Galilee** is a freshwater lake in the north-center of the map, west of the Transjordanian plateau.
4. **Egypt** is to the south and southwest (bottom-left). The Nile Delta faces the Mediterranean.
5. **Mesopotamia / Assyria / Babylon** is to the northeast and east (top-right and right edge). The Euphrates and Tigris rivers are off the right edge or at the far-right margin.
6. **Phoenicia** (Tyre, Sidon) is to the north along the coast, above the Israelite territory.
7. The **Philistine cities** (Gaza, Ashkelon, Ashdod, Ekron, Gath) are on the southern coastal plain, between the Mediterranean and the Judean hills.
8. The **central hill country** runs north-south through the center of the map, between the coastal plain and the Jordan Rift Valley. Jerusalem, Bethel, Shechem, and Samaria sit on or near this ridge.
9. **Transjordan** (Ammon, Moab, Edom) is east of the Jordan River and the Dead Sea, on the plateau to the right side of the map.
