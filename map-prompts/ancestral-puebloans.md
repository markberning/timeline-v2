## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL ANCESTRAL PUEBLOANS PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this. Every word on the final map must appear verbatim somewhere in this prompt.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Rivers and lakes are light blue. Desert/arid land is pale yellow/tan. Forested uplands are soft green.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**FOUR CORNERS ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The **Four Corners** is where Utah (NW), Colorado (NE), Arizona (SW), and New Mexico (SE) meet. This is the center of the Ancestral Puebloan world.
3. The **Colorado Plateau** is a high, arid tableland covering the Four Corners region and beyond. It is mesa-and-canyon country, mostly 5,000–8,000 ft elevation.
4. **Chaco Canyon** is in NORTHWESTERN New Mexico, in the San Juan Basin. It runs roughly east-west. It is SOUTH of the San Juan River and SOUTH of Mesa Verde.
5. **Mesa Verde** is in SOUTHWESTERN Colorado, roughly 90 miles (145 km) NORTH of Chaco Canyon.
6. The **San Juan River** flows WEST through the Four Corners region, NORTH of Chaco Canyon, joining the Colorado River in southeastern Utah.
7. The **Rio Grande** is FAR to the EAST and SOUTH of the Four Corners, flowing SOUTH through central New Mexico. It is the destination of the great migration after the Four Corners were abandoned.
8. The **Chuska Mountains** are WEST of Chaco Canyon, along the New Mexico–Arizona border. The **San Mateo Mountains** and **Jemez Mountains** are SOUTH and SOUTHEAST of Chaco Canyon. These are the timber sources for Chacoan construction.
9. The **Hopi Mesas** are in NORTHEASTERN Arizona, west-southwest of Mesa Verde.
10. **Zuni** is on the Arizona–New Mexico border, south of the Four Corners.
