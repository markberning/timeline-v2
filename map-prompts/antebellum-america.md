## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL ANTEBELLUM AMERICA PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in this prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name. If this prompt says "Charleston (secession)", the rendered label must begin with "Charleston" — not just "secession".
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation. Do not rotate or tilt the map.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are rendered in light blue. Do not render water as tan, cream, or white. Land is beige/tan. Mountains are light brown.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.** If it is not in the prompt, do not draw it.

**UNITED STATES ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top of the map. East is to the right. The **Atlantic Ocean** is the right (eastern) edge. The **Gulf of Mexico** is the bottom (southern) edge. The far west is the left edge.
2. The **Appalachian Mountains** run NORTHEAST-to-SOUTHWEST as a long ridge system in the eastern interior, the barrier between the Atlantic seaboard and the interior. They are NEVER on the coast and NEVER run east-to-west.
3. The **Mississippi River** is the central north–south line, flowing from the Great Lakes region south to the Gulf of Mexico at New Orleans. It does NOT run east-to-west.
4. The **Ohio River** is the practical free/slave boundary: free states lie NORTH of the Ohio River and the 36°30′ line, slave states lie SOUTH of it. The Ohio joins the Mississippi from the east.
5. The **Missouri Compromise line at 36°30′** runs east–west across the former Louisiana territory west of Missouri: free above it, slave below it.
6. The **Great Lakes** are at the top-center. The **Rio Grande** and **Texas** are the southwest. The **Mexican Cession** (1848) and **Oregon** are the far west.
7. Key places: **Washington D.C.** (mid-Atlantic seaboard), **Charleston** (South Carolina coast), **New Orleans** (mouth of the Mississippi on the Gulf), the **Cotton Kingdom** belt arcing South Carolina → Georgia → Alabama → Mississippi → Louisiana → Texas, the **Cherokee / Creek / Choctaw / Chickasaw / Seminole** Southeast homelands and **Indian Territory** (Oklahoma, west of Arkansas), the **Erie Canal** across New York, **Lowell** in Massachusetts, **Nauvoo** in Illinois and the Mormon trail to the **Great Salt Lake**, **Kansas** and **Nebraska** territories, **Harpers Ferry** in Virginia, **Fort Sumter** in Charleston harbor.
