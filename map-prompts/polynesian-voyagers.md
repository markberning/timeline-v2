## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL POLYNESIAN VOYAGERS PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any island name, region name, ocean label, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this. Every word on the final map must appear verbatim somewhere in this prompt.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Ocean, seas, lagoons, and rivers are light blue. Land is beige/tan or green for forested islands. Coral atolls are very small.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**PACIFIC ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. The Pacific Ocean fills MOST of the map. Land is tiny — islands are dots or small shapes in an enormous ocean.
2. **Australia and Papua New Guinea** are at the FAR LEFT (west) edge of the map. They are large landmasses that anchor the western side.
3. **South America** is at the FAR RIGHT (east) edge of the map, if shown at all.
4. The **Polynesian Triangle** has three corners: **Hawai'i** at the top (north), **Easter Island / Rapa Nui** at the bottom-right (southeast), and **New Zealand / Aotearoa** at the bottom-left (southwest). This triangle spans roughly 10 million square miles of ocean.
5. **Western Polynesia** (Fiji, Tonga, Samoa) is in the LEFT-CENTER of the map, east of Papua New Guinea / Melanesia.
6. **Eastern Polynesia** (Marquesas, Tahiti, Cook Islands) is in the CENTER-RIGHT of the map.
7. North is at the top. The equator runs roughly through the middle of the map horizontally.
8. Islands are TINY relative to the ocean. Do not exaggerate island sizes. The ocean dominance is the entire point.
