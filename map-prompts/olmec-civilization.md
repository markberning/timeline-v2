## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL OLMEC PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this. Every word on the final map must appear verbatim somewhere in this prompt.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, and rivers are light blue. Deserts are pale yellow. Land is beige/tan. Tropical lowlands are soft green.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**MESOAMERICA ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The Gulf of Mexico is to the EAST and NORTHEAST. The Pacific Ocean is to the SOUTH and SOUTHWEST. Do not swap these.
3. The Olmec heartland is in the Gulf Coast lowlands of the modern Mexican states of Veracruz and Tabasco, on the EASTERN side of Mexico, facing the Gulf.
4. The Tuxtla Mountains (Sierra de los Tuxtlas, a volcanic range) are NORTHWEST of San Lorenzo, inland from the Gulf Coast.
5. San Lorenzo, La Venta, and Tres Zapotes are in a roughly 120-mile (200 km) strip along the Gulf Coast lowlands. San Lorenzo is the SOUTHERNMOST of the three. Tres Zapotes is the NORTHWESTERNMOST, on the slopes of the Tuxtla Mountains. La Venta is NORTHEAST of San Lorenzo, closer to the coast, in Tabasco.
6. The Valley of Mexico (site of Tlatilco, later Teotihuacan) is approximately 250 miles (400 km) to the NORTHWEST, in the central highlands at high elevation.
7. The Motagua Valley in Guatemala (the jade source) is approximately 600 miles (1,000 km) to the SOUTHEAST.
8. Oaxaca (Valley of Oaxaca, site of Monte Alban) is to the SOUTHWEST, in the highlands.
9. Guerrero (site of Juxtlahuaca and Oxtotitlan caves) is to the SOUTHWEST, on the Pacific side.
10. The Coatzacoalcos River and the Tonala River flow through the Olmec heartland from the highlands into the Gulf of Mexico.
