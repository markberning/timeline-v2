**CHINA ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The Yellow River (Huang He) is the MAIN northern river, flowing west-to-east with a large loop around the Ordos Desert. The Yangtze (Chang Jiang) is the MAIN southern river, flowing west-to-east further south.
3. The Loess Plateau is in the NORTH-CENTRAL region (Shaanxi, Shanxi, northern Henan). It is NEVER east of central Henan and NEVER south of the Qinling Mountains.
4. The Yi River and Luo River are small tributaries that join the Yellow River from the south near modern Luoyang. They are NOT the Yellow River itself — they are thin lines branching up from the south, not the main horizontal river line.

**ADDITIONAL TANG-SONG ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. Chang'an (modern Xi'an) is on the Wei River in the north-central interior, west of Luoyang. Luoyang is east of Chang'an. Both sit just south of the great east-west bend of the Yellow River.
2. Kaifeng is east of Luoyang on the lower Yellow River. Hangzhou is far south on the southeast coast at the mouth of the Qiantang River.
3. The Grand Canal runs roughly NORTH-SOUTH, linking Hangzhou in the south to the area of modern Beijing in the north, crossing the Yangtze and Yellow Rivers en route.
4. Korea is to the EAST. The Korean peninsula is across the Yellow Sea from Shandong.
5. Central Asia / the Tarim Basin is to the WEST. Mongolia / the steppe is to the NORTH. Vietnam / Champa is to the FAR SOUTH.
