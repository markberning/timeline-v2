**DELHI SULTANATE ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The Indian subcontinent is the main landmass — a triangular peninsula bounded by the Himalayas to the north, the Arabian Sea to the west, and the Bay of Bengal to the east.
3. Delhi is in the NORTH of India, on the Yamuna river, at the strategic crux between the Khyber Pass invasion corridor and the Gangetic plain.
4. The Yamuna river flows from the Himalayas south-southeast through Delhi and joins the Ganges at Allahabad. The Ganges flows from the Himalayas east-southeast across the northern plain to Bengal.
5. The Indus river flows from the Himalayas south through the Punjab and Sindh into the Arabian Sea (in modern Pakistan). The Khyber Pass is in the northwest, between Afghanistan and the Punjab.
6. The Deccan Plateau is the central-southern interior of the subcontinent, separated from the northern plain by the Vindhya and Satpura ranges. The Krishna and Godavari rivers cross the Deccan from west to east into the Bay of Bengal.
7. Madurai is in the far SOUTH of the subcontinent, near the southern tip; Kashmir is in the far NORTH. The straight-line distance between them is roughly 1,500 miles.
8. Bengal is the eastern Ganges delta region (modern Bangladesh and West Bengal). Gujarat is the western coastal region on the Arabian Sea. Rajasthan is the dry northwestern interior.
9. Lahore is in the upper Punjab (modern Pakistan). Multan is in the lower Punjab. Kabul and Ghazni are in eastern Afghanistan.
