## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL ANGLO-SAXON ENGLAND PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, and rivers are light blue. Land is beige/tan. Mountains are light brown.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**ANGLO-SAXON ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The map is primarily the island of **Britain**: England and Wales occupying the southern two-thirds, Scotland the northern third.
3. Where relevant, the **North Sea** is to the EAST, with the Anglo-Saxon homelands — the Jutland peninsula of Denmark (the Jutes), and the coast of northern Germany/Frisia (the Angles and Saxons) — visible across the water to the EAST-NORTHEAST.
4. The kingdoms by position on the island: **Kent** is in the far SOUTHEAST corner. **Sussex** is on the SOUTH coast just west of Kent. **Wessex** is the SOUTH and SOUTHWEST. **East Anglia** is the eastern bulge north of the Thames estuary. **Mercia** is the landlocked MIDLANDS and CENTER. **Northumbria** (formed from Bernicia in the north and Deira to its south) occupies the NORTH of England. The Britons/Wales are to the WEST, Cornwall at the far SOUTHWEST tip.
5. Key places by position: **Canterbury** is in Kent (far SE). **Sutton Hoo** is in East Anglia on the River Deben near the coast. **Lindisfarne** is a tidal island on the Northumbrian NE coast. **Bamburgh** is on the Northumbrian NE coast. **Jarrow/Wearmouth** are on the River Tyne in Northumbria (NE coast). **Whitby** is on the North Yorkshire coast. **Offa's Dyke** runs along the WELSH BORDER (the western frontier of Mercia). The **Thames** flows roughly east through southern England into the North Sea. The **Humber** estuary is the great seam between northern and southern England (Northumbria to its north, the Midlands/south to its south). **Hadrian's Wall** runs east-west across the far NORTH of England just south of the Scottish border.
6. Trade emporia (**wics**): **Hamwic** is near modern Southampton on the south coast. **Lundenwic** is on the Thames (London area). **Ipswich** is on the East Anglian coast.
