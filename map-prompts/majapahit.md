## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL MAJAPAHIT PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in this prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are rendered in light blue. Do not render water as tan, cream, or white. Land is beige/tan. River floodplains are soft green.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.** Do NOT label any river, mountain range, or sea with a name not listed in this prompt.

The header bar is the ONLY bordered element of the image. The left, right, and bottom edges of the image have NO border or frame — the map geography extends fully to all three of those edges, edge-to-edge. Do not draw a rectangular frame around the map on the left, right, or bottom sides.

**MAJAPAHIT ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right. Java is a long, thin volcanic island running roughly WEST-to-EAST. The Java Sea is the water NORTH of Java; the Indian Ocean is the open water SOUTH of Java.
2. East Java, the Brantas River valley, is the Majapahit heartland. The capital Trowulan sits inland in the lower Brantas plain near modern Mojokerto, a short way south of the north coast. Central Java (Borobudur, Prambanan) lies WEST of East Java. West Java is Sunda.
3. Bali is the island immediately EAST of Java's eastern tip, across a narrow strait. Madura is the long island just off the NORTHEAST coast of East Java. Lombok is the next island EAST of Bali.
4. Sumatra is the large island to the WEST, across the Sunda Strait. Borneo (Kalimantan) is to the NORTH across the Java Sea. The Malay Peninsula and the Strait of Malacca are far to the NORTHWEST. The Maluku spice islands are far to the EAST. Palembang is on southeast Sumatra.
5. The north-coast ports — Tuban, Gresik, and Surabaya — sit on the north shore of East Java facing the Java Sea. Demak is a north-coast port in north-central Java. Kediri and Daha are inland up the Brantas; Singhasari is inland East Java near modern Malang.
6. The Brantas River runs through East Java from the southern volcanic highlands, looping north past Kediri toward the Java Sea near Surabaya. Draw it as a curving river entirely within East Java, not a coast and not a strait.
