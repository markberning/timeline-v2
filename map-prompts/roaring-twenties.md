## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL ROARING TWENTIES PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in this prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name. If this prompt says "Seattle (general strike)", the rendered label must begin with "Seattle" — not just "general strike".
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation. Do not rotate or tilt the map.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are rendered in light blue. Do not render water as tan, cream, or white. Land is beige/tan. Mountains are light brown.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.** If it is not in the prompt, do not draw it.

1. North is at the top of the map. East is to the right. The **Atlantic Ocean** is the right (eastern) edge. The **Gulf of Mexico** is the bottom (southern) edge. The **Pacific Ocean** is the left (western) edge.
2. The **Appalachian Mountains** run NORTHEAST-to-SOUTHWEST as a long ridge system in the eastern interior. They are NEVER on the coast and NEVER run east-to-west.
3. The **Mississippi River** is the central north–south line, flowing from the Great Lakes region south to the Gulf of Mexico at New Orleans. It does NOT run east-to-west.
4. The **Great Lakes** are at the top-center. The **Rocky Mountains** are the western interior spine.
