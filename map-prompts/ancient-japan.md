## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL ANCIENT JAPAN PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, and rivers are light blue. Deserts are pale yellow. Land is beige/tan.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**JAPAN ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The Japanese archipelago runs in a rough arc from **northeast** (Hokkaido) to **southwest** (Kyushu and the Ryukyu Islands). The four main islands from north to south are: Hokkaido, Honshu (the largest), Shikoku, and Kyushu.
3. The **Sea of Japan** is to the WEST/NORTHWEST of the archipelago, separating Japan from the Korean Peninsula and mainland Asia. The **Pacific Ocean** is to the EAST/SOUTHEAST.
4. The **Korean Peninsula** is to the WEST, across the Korea Strait (~120 miles / 200 km from Kyushu).
5. **Kyushu** is the SOUTHWESTERN main island, closest to Korea. **Hokkaido** is the NORTHEASTERN main island.
6. The **Kinai region** (around modern Nara and Osaka) is in SOUTH-CENTRAL Honshu.
7. **Kamakura** is in EASTERN Honshu, near modern Tokyo.
