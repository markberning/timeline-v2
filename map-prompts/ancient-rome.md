## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL ANCIENT ROME PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this. Every word on the final map must appear verbatim somewhere in this prompt.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, and rivers are light blue. Deserts are pale yellow. Land is beige/tan.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**ROMAN ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. **Rome** is on the **Tiber River** in central Italy, roughly 16 miles (26 km) inland from the western coast. Italy is the long boot-shaped peninsula running NW-to-SE in the center of the Mediterranean.
3. **Etruria** (Etruscan territory) is NORTH of Rome, in what is now Tuscany.
4. **Magna Graecia** ("Greater Greece") is the Greek-colonized region of SOUTHERN Italy and eastern Sicily.
5. **Carthage** is on the coast of modern Tunisia, roughly 400 miles (640 km) SOUTH of Rome across the Mediterranean.
6. The **Alps** are the high mountain range at the NORTH of the Italian peninsula separating it from Gaul and Germania.
7. **Gaul** (modern France) is WEST and NORTHWEST of the Alps. **Germania** is NORTHEAST of the Alps.
8. **Constantinople** is at the Bosporus strait, far to the EAST of Rome, controlling the passage between Europe and Asia.
9. The **Rhine** and **Danube** rivers form the empire's northern frontier, running roughly west-to-east.
