## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL MAURYA EMPIRE PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text. (On prior runs Gemini invented words like "cononniat", "reachit", and "Kushi" — do not do this. Every word on the final map must appear verbatim somewhere in this prompt.)
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, and rivers are light blue. Deserts are pale yellow. Land is beige/tan.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**INDIA ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. The **Himalayas** run along the top edge as a mountain barrier, curving from northwest to east.
2. The **Punjab** ("Land of Five Rivers") is in the NORTHWEST — five rivers (Jhelum, Chenab, Ravi, Beas, Sutlej) flow roughly southwest from the Himalayas to join the Indus.
3. The **Indus River** is on the WESTERN side of the map, flowing south to the Arabian Sea. It is NOT in central India.
4. The **Ganges River** flows WEST-to-EAST across northern India, roughly 600 miles south of the Himalayas, emptying into the Bay of Bengal in the east.
5. The **Yamuna River** flows roughly parallel to the Ganges but west of it, joining the Ganges near modern Allahabad (Prayagraj). The land between them is the **Ganges-Yamuna doab**.
6. The **Deccan Plateau** occupies the southern half of India, south of the Narmada River.
7. The **Arabian Sea** is to the WEST (left side of map). The **Bay of Bengal** is to the EAST (right side of map).
8. The **Hindu Kush** mountains are in the FAR NORTHWEST, beyond the Punjab, forming the gateway between Central Asia and the subcontinent.
