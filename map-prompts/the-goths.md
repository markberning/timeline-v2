## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL THE GOTHS PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are light blue. Land is beige/tan. Mountains are light brown.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**GOTHIC ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The Gothic arc moves from the lower **Vistula** (modern Poland, Wielbark homeland) SOUTHEAST to the Black Sea / Pontic steppe (modern Ukraine and Romania, Chernyakhov zone).
3. The **Danube** is the Roman frontier, running roughly west-to-east before bending south through modern Romania. Everything SOUTH of the lower Danube is Roman territory.
4. The **Balkans** and **Thrace** are SOUTH of the lower Danube. **Constantinople** is at the far southeast, on the strait between the Black Sea and the Aegean.
5. **Adrianople** is in Thrace, roughly 130 miles (210 km) northwest of Constantinople — it is NOT on the coast.
6. **Italy** (Rome, Ravenna) is far to the WEST, across the Adriatic Sea.
7. **Gaul** (Toulouse) and **Hispania** (Toledo) are far to the WEST and SOUTHWEST of Italy.
8. The **Catalaunian Plains** are in northeastern Gaul (modern Champagne region of France).
9. Key waters from north to south: Baltic Sea (north) → Vistula River (Poland) → Danube River (frontier) → Adriatic Sea (west of Italy) → Black Sea (east) → Aegean Sea (south of Thrace) → Mediterranean Sea.
