## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL GERMANIC TRIBES PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in the prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, and rivers are light blue. Land is beige/tan. Mountains are light brown.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**GERMANIC ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The **Rhine** river runs roughly north–south and is the WESTERN frontier of Germania. **Gaul** (modern France) is WEST of the Rhine. **Germania** is EAST of the Rhine.
3. The **Danube** runs roughly west–east and is the SOUTHERN frontier of Germania. **Italy** is SOUTH across the Alps from the Danube. **Roman territory** is SOUTH and WEST of the Rhine–Danube line.
4. The **Germanic homeland** (Jastorf culture) is the NORTH GERMAN PLAIN, the JUTLAND PENINSULA (modern Denmark), and SOUTHERN SCANDINAVIA — all in the far north of the map.
5. The **Elbe** and **Weser** rivers run roughly south-to-north through the interior of Germania, west of the Oder. The **Elbe** is east of the Weser.
6. The **Teutoburg Forest** / **Kalkriese** site is in NORTHWEST Germany, between the Rhine and the Weser.
7. The **Black Sea** and the lower Danube are FAR to the SOUTHEAST. The Goths arrive there late; do not place them in the northwest.
8. The **Alps** run east–west, separating the Danube frontier from Italy. They appear in the south-center of any pan-Germanic map.
