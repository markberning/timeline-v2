## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL ENLIGHTENMENT PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in this prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are rendered in light blue. Do not render water as tan, cream, or white. Land is beige/tan; mountains are soft brown.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

---

The chapter title "Chapter N: <Title>" should appear at the top of the map image, centered, in a clean serif font, inside a header bar that spans the full width of the image. The header bar is the ONLY bordered element of the image. The left, right, and bottom edges of the image have NO border or frame — the map geography extends fully to all three of those edges, edge-to-edge. Do not draw a rectangular frame around the map on the left, right, or bottom sides.

**EUROPE ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right. The Atlantic Ocean is on the west (left). The Mediterranean Sea is to the south.
2. England is the island in the northwest, across the English Channel from France. London is in southeastern England; Cambridge and Oxford are near London; Edinburgh and Glasgow are in Scotland to the north.
3. France is the large country south of the English Channel. Paris is in north-central France on the Seine; Versailles is just southwest of Paris; Bordeaux is in southwestern France; Ferney and Geneva are on the Swiss border in the east.
4. The Dutch Republic (the Netherlands) is the low coastal country northeast of France. Amsterdam, Leiden, and Rotterdam are Dutch cities.
5. The German lands and Prussia are the patchwork in the center and northeast. Berlin and Potsdam are in Prussia; Königsberg is far northeast on the Baltic; Vienna is southeast in Austria.
6. St. Petersburg is far to the northeast, on the Baltic in Russia. Naples is in southern Italy.
7. The Atlantic colonies — North America and the Caribbean — lie far to the west, across the Atlantic Ocean.
