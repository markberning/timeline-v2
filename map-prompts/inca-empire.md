## GLOBAL RULES FOR ALL INCA EMPIRE PROMPTS — read before drawing any map in this file

**CRITICAL RULES — read carefully before drawing:**

1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in this prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are light blue. Deserts are pale yellow. Land is beige/tan. Highland terrain is light brown.
6. **Do not invent any labels, annotations, sites, or features that are not in this prompt.**

**ANDES ORIENTATION RULES (Inca version):**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The **Pacific Ocean** is to the WEST (left side of the map). The **Andes mountains** run NORTH-TO-SOUTH near the western coast. The **Amazon basin** is to the EAST.
3. The empire stretched roughly 2,500 miles north-to-south. **Quito** is at the NORTHERN end (modern Ecuador). **Cuzco** is in the south-central highlands of modern Peru. The **Maule River** is the SOUTHERN frontier in central Chile.
4. **Lake Titicaca** is in the SOUTH, on the modern Peru-Bolivia border. **Tiwanaku** ruins are on its southern shore.
5. **Machu Picchu** is in the cloud forest northwest of Cuzco, in the Sacred Valley.
6. **Cajamarca** is in the northern highlands of Peru, between Cuzco and Quito.
7. **Tumipampa** (modern Cuenca) is in the southern highlands of modern Ecuador, north of the Peru-Ecuador border.
