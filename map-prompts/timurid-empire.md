**TIMURID EMPIRE ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The Caspian Sea is in the WEST-CENTRAL part of the map — a landlocked lake with the Caucasus on its west shore and Iran on its south shore.
3. The Aral Sea is to the NORTHEAST of the Caspian — a smaller landlocked lake fed by the Amu Darya and Syr Darya rivers.
4. The Amu Darya flows from the southeast (Hindu Kush sources) to the northwest into the Aral Sea. The Syr Darya flows from the east (Tian Shan sources) to the northwest into the Aral Sea. Both rivers run roughly parallel.
5. **Transoxiana** is the agricultural region BETWEEN the Amu Darya and Syr Darya rivers. **Samarkand** and **Bukhara** are both in Transoxiana, NORTH of the Amu Darya.
6. **Khorasan** is the region SOUTH of the Amu Darya, covering northeastern Iran and northwestern Afghanistan. **Herat** is in Khorasan, southwest of Samarkand.
7. The Hindu Kush mountain range runs east-west through central Afghanistan. **Kabul** is in eastern Afghanistan, on the Indian-subcontinent side of the Hindu Kush.
8. **Tabriz** is in northwestern Iran, near the southern Caucasus. **Baghdad** is in Mesopotamia (modern Iraq), west of the Zagros Mountains. **Damascus** and **Aleppo** are in Syria, on the Mediterranean coast region.
9. **Delhi** is in northern India, far to the southeast of Kabul, across the Khyber Pass and the Punjab plain.
10. **Ankara** is in central Anatolia (modern Turkey), far to the northwest. **Constantinople** is on the Bosphorus at the western edge of Anatolia.
