**MEIJI JAPAN ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right.
2. The four main Japanese islands run roughly NORTHEAST-to-SOUTHWEST. From north to south: Hokkaido (largest northern island), Honshu (the main central island, longest), Shikoku (smaller, southeast of western Honshu), Kyushu (southernmost main island).
3. The Sea of Japan is to the WEST of the Japanese islands. The Pacific Ocean is to the EAST.
4. Tokyo (formerly Edo) is on the eastern coast of central Honshu, on Tokyo Bay (formerly Edo Bay). Kyoto is in west-central Honshu. Osaka is southwest of Kyoto on Osaka Bay. Nagasaki is on the western coast of Kyushu. Kagoshima is on the southern coast of Kyushu. Sapporo is in central-southern Hokkaido. Hakodate is on the southern tip of Hokkaido.
5. The Korean Peninsula is to the WEST across the Sea of Japan. The Tsushima Strait runs between southern Korea and northwestern Kyushu.
6. The Asian mainland (Korea, Manchuria, Russian Far East) is to the WEST and NORTHWEST. Taiwan is far to the SOUTHWEST, off the southern coast of China.
7. Mainland Japan and the major cities are densely packed; do not show every minor island. The map should focus on the four main islands.
