1. **Draw each label exactly once.** Do not repeat any city name, region name, river name, or annotation anywhere on the map. If you see an opportunity to put the same label in two places, choose only one.
2. **Spell every label exactly as written in this prompt.** Do not invent words. Do not abbreviate. Do not generate any word that is not in the prompt text.
3. **Include the exact site name on every label.** The site name is the word or phrase before the first parenthesis or em-dash. Never drop a site name.
4. **North is at the top of the map. East is to the right.** Place every location according to its real-world geography in this orientation.
5. **All water is light blue.** Seas, bays, lakes, and rivers are rendered in light blue. Do not render water as tan, cream, or white. Land is beige/tan. River floodplains are soft green.

**VIETNAM ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right. Vietnam is a long, thin country running down the eastern edge of mainland Southeast Asia, facing the South China Sea to the east.
2. The Red River delta is in the FAR NORTH (around Hanoi/Thăng Long and the Gulf of Tonkin). The Mekong Delta is in the FAR SOUTH (around Saigon and the Cà Mau point). The country is pinched narrow in the CENTER, around Huế.
3. China is to the NORTH, beyond the Red River delta. The Gulf of Tonkin is the bay in the NORTHEAST; the South China Sea is the open sea to the EAST; the Gulf of Thailand is to the far SOUTHWEST.
4. The Annamite Range (Trường Sơn) is a long mountain chain running roughly NORTH–SOUTH down the spine of the country, separating the narrow Vietnamese coastal strip (east) from Laos and the Cambodian interior (west).
5. Champa occupied the CENTRAL coast (its capital Vijaya is near modern Quy Nhơn). Cambodia and the Tonlé Sap / lower Mekong are to the SOUTHWEST. Laos (Lan Xang) is WEST, across the Annamite Range.
6. Thăng Long (Hanoi) is in the Red River delta in the north. Hoa Lư is just SOUTH of the delta. Huế / Phú Xuân is in the narrow center. Hội An is just south of Huế. Đồng Hới is on the narrow waist. Saigon (Prey Nokor) is in the south near the Mekong. Hà Tiên is on the Gulf of Thailand in the far southwest.
