**MALI EMPIRE ORIENTATION RULES:**

1. North is at the top. East is to the right. The Atlantic Ocean is on the western (left) edge of the map. The Sahara Desert covers the upper (northern) half. The Sahel band runs east-to-west across the middle. The savanna and forest belt is at the bottom (south).
2. The Niger River bends NORTH from the Guinea highlands into the Sahel, runs EAST past Timbuktu in a great east-west arc, then turns SOUTHEAST at the Niger Bend toward modern Nigeria. The Niger does NOT run straight east-to-west; the great northern arc is the defining shape of the river.
3. Timbuktu sits on the NORTHERN side of the Niger River bend, on the southern edge of the Sahara. Djenné sits SOUTHWEST of Timbuktu in the Inner Niger Delta. Gao sits EAST of Timbuktu, near the start of the southeast turn. Niani sits SOUTHWEST of Djenné, in the upper Niger headwaters in modern Guinea.
4. The Senegal River runs WEST from the Bambuk gold field region to the Atlantic coast. The Niger and Senegal rivers both rise in the Guinea highlands but flow in opposite directions.
5. The Inner Niger Delta is a large flooded grassland zone in the central Niger arc, between Djenné and Timbuktu. It is NOT a coastal river delta; it is an inland seasonal flood plain.
6. Morocco / the Maghreb is at the upper LEFT (northwest), separated from the Sahel by the Sahara. Egypt is at the upper RIGHT (northeast), reached from Mali by trans-Saharan caravans across modern Algeria, Niger, and Libya. Mecca is far east, in the Arabian Peninsula.
7. The Bambuk gold field is on the upper Senegal River, between modern Senegal and Mali. The Bure gold field is on the upper Niger River, in modern Guinea. Taghaza is a salt-mining oasis deep in the central Sahara, far north of Timbuktu.
