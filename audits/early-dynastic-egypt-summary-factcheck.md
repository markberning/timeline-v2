## Chapter 5 — Narmer Unifies Egypt

1. `[STRONG]` **Bullet:** "Hor-Aha, Narmer's successor, raided Nubia, expanded the temple of Neith at Sais in the Delta, and consolidated the new state by fighting on all its frontiers." — **Issue:** The narrative does not say Hor-Aha "expanded the temple of Neith at Sais." It says a label from his tomb "shows a boat with a shrine, captioned with the name of the goddess Neith," and explains that Neith was a warrior goddess from Sais — but never claims Hor-Aha expanded or built a temple there. — **Narrative says:** "One label shows a boat with a shrine, captioned with the name of the goddess Neith (a warrior goddess from the city of Sais in the western Delta — her cult center was deep in Lower Egyptian territory, and its prominence under the First Dynasty kings is a sign that the new dynasty was making active efforts to integrate the conquered north)."

Rest of Chapter 5 bullets are clean.

## Chapter 6 — The First Dynasty: Building the Pharaonic System

1. `[CHECK]` **Bullet:** "Den reigned about 40 years and standardized the pharaonic toolkit: the Two Ladies name, the Heb Sed jubilee run, the smiting pose, the first census, and the nome system of 42 provinces." — **Issue:** Two dropped qualifiers. The narrative says the census was "probably the first organized census" and the nome system had "roughly 42 administrative units." The bullet states both as unqualified facts. — **Narrative says:** "He conducted what his labels call the 'first time of counting' — *probably* the first organized census of livestock and grain" and "the Egyptian state was divided into *roughly* 42 administrative units called nomes."

2. `[STRONG]` **Bullet:** "An ivory label from Den's tomb shows the first depiction of the Heb Sed jubilee running ritual, the barefoot sprint between boundary stones that proved the king was still fit to rule." — **Issue:** "Barefoot" is not in the narrative. The narrative calls it "a ceremonial run between boundary stones" and says the figure was "wearing the short jubilee kilt." The word "sprint" also upgrades the narrative's "run." — **Narrative says:** "a ceremonial run between boundary stones... it shows a figure (probably Den himself) running between two sets of markers, wearing the short jubilee kilt."

3. `[CHECK]` **Bullet:** "First Dynasty expeditions to Sinai for turquoise and copper left rock inscriptions at Wadi Maghara, the earliest royal texts outside the Nile Valley." — **Issue:** Drops "among" qualifier, making the claim more definitive than the narrative. — **Narrative says:** "These Sinai inscriptions are *among the very earliest* royal texts outside the Nile Valley."

Rest of Chapter 6 bullets are clean.
