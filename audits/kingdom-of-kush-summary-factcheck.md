## Chapter 6 — Iron, Script, and Queens

1. `[CHECK]` **Bullet:** "The Meroitic script, invented around 300 BCE, was the first indigenous writing system south of the Sahara, an alphabetic abugida in both hieroglyphic and cursive forms that encoded the Kushite spoken language." — **Issue:** The bullet calls the script "an alphabetic abugida." The narrative carefully identifies it as "an abugida" without the word "alphabetic." An abugida is a distinct script category from an alphabet; conflating them loses a meaningful technical distinction. — **Narrative says:** "The Meroitic script is technically an abugida — a writing system in which each symbol represents a consonant with an inherent vowel, and additional marks modify the vowel."
