# Term-Precision Sweep — cross-civ tracker

A *future, full-scale* pass: find places where a narrative introduces a term/region/label
**imprecisely enough that a reasonable reader is misled**, and add a short in-voice
clarification (parenthetical, per WRITING-RULES). The Scandinavia≠Nordic case is the
first instance found; it is one member of a broader class.

The class: a familiar word used in its *strict* sense without flagging that the reader's
*loose* sense is wrong (or vice versa). Examples of the pattern to hunt for later:
"Scandinavia" vs "Nordic", "England" vs "Britain" vs "UK", "Holland" vs "Netherlands",
"Persia" vs "Iran" vs "the Persian Empire", "Byzantine" vs "Roman", "America(s)",
"Asia Minor"/"Anatolia"/"Turkey", "Celtic", "Aryan/Indo-Aryan", "Frank"/"French",
"Mongol"/"Tatar", "Arab"/"Muslim"/"Islamic", "Egypt" (ancient vs modern), "India"
(subcontinent vs republic). Each needs a one-clause pre-emption, not a rewrite.

## 1. Scandinavia ≠ Nordic (Finland/Iceland) — instance #1

**Trigger:** user reading viking-age Ch1 asked "isn't Finland Scandinavian?" — the
text was correct (Scandinavia = DK/NO/SE; Nordic adds Finland+Iceland; Finland is
Finnic, not Norse) but the strict usage invited the question.

### Fixed
- ✅ **viking-age** Ch1 — added a 2-sentence parenthetical clarification
  (Scandinavia proper = North Germanic core; Nordic is wider; Viking-Age Finland
  was Finnic and sits outside the story). Committed.
