# Persona A (Curious Newcomer) Audit — Early Dynastic Egypt

## Chapter 1 — The Green Sahara and the Refugees

1. **"feedback loops kicked in (less vegetation meant less local moisture meant less rain meant less vegetation)"** — The mechanism that converts less vegetation into less local moisture is not explained. — How does removing plants reduce moisture? What is the physical process?

2. **"the most famous monsoon is the South Asian one"** — The parenthetical on monsoons runs 80+ words and derails the sentence about why the Sahara was green. — Long parenthetical breaks reading flow.

3. **"Over very long timescales, the way Earth tilts and orbits the Sun changes in slow regular cycles."** — These orbital cycles are asserted but not given a name or any concrete sense of their timescale. — How long is one cycle? Are these the Milankovitch cycles?

4. **"(This is unintuitive enough that it is worth explaining: the headwaters of the Nile are in equatorial central Africa...)"** — This parenthetical is 130+ words. It is useful information but breaks the sentence it is attached to. — Long parenthetical disrupts flow.

5. **"indigenous Nile fisher-foragers"** — The people who were already at the Nile before the Saharan refugees are mentioned but never described. — What did they eat, how did they live, how many were there?

6. **"small bands moving down the river from south, north, and along the Mediterranean coast from the Levant"** — The groups from the south and from the Mediterranean coast are mentioned but given zero detail. — Who were these people? What culture did they belong to?

7. **"Five regions of the world were running the same experiment in parallel"** — Only four regions are named in the preceding paragraphs (Nile, Mesopotamia, Indus, Yellow River). The fifth is not identified. — Which is the fifth region?

## Chapter 2 — Badarians and the Birth of an Egyptian Look

8. **"the Badarian phase shades into the next phase of Upper Egyptian culture, which archaeologists call Naqada I or the Amratian. The transition is gradual"** — "Gradual" is asserted but given no concrete indication of how many years or what changed first. — How gradual? A century? Five centuries? What signals the transition?

9. **"Badarian pottery doesn't disappear so much as evolve into Naqada I pottery"** — What specifically changes in the pottery? The reader is told the Badarian look but not what evolved. — What is the difference between a late Badarian pot and an early Naqada I pot?

10. **"Hatshepsut, Sobekneferu, Tausret, and (in a different mode) Cleopatra"** — Four names dropped with no dates, context, or explanation of who they are. — Who are these women? When did they rule?

11. **"rippled ware"** — Mentioned as the "highest-status pottery" but no explanation of why rippled texture would signal higher status. — Why would finger ridges make a pot more valuable?

12. **"The earliest horizontal ground looms — flat looms staked into the ground, with the warp threads stretched between two beams — appear in Egypt around this time"** — The term "warp threads" is not defined. — What are warp threads?

13. **"true mummification with deliberate dehydration was still a thousand years in the future"** — What does "deliberate dehydration" involve? — How did they dehydrate a body?

14. **"the Sahara migration: Upper Egypt may have absorbed more refugees from the drying western desert than the Delta did"** — Why would Upper Egypt absorb more refugees than the Delta? No geographic or logistical reason is given. — Why would refugees prefer the narrow southern valley over the wide northern delta?

## Chapter 3 — Naqada I and II: Upper Egypt Pulls Ahead

15. **"Sir Flinders Petrie excavated a massive predynastic cemetery in 1894 and used the pottery sequence he found there to define the chronology"** — Process described by result only. How does sorting pottery into groups give you a chronological sequence? — How do you get a timeline from pot shapes?

16. **"standards (long poles topped with the symbol of a particular community or god — the same standards that will later appear in royal processions and in the iconography of the nomes)"** — The word "nomes" is used here before it is defined. It is not defined until Chapter 6. — What is a nome?

17. **"natron (a naturally occurring sodium compound used as a flux)"** — The word "flux" is not defined. — What does a flux do in the faience-making process?

18. **"sewn-plank boats: hulls made of cedar or acacia planks"** — Where did the cedar come from? Cedar is not native to Egypt — this is not explained until Chapter 6 (Byblos trade). — How did predynastic Egyptians get cedar?

19. **"Egyptian elites had become wealthy enough to participate in the proto-globalized trade networks of the Bronze Age"** — The phrase "Bronze Age" is used without explanation. We are told about copper but not about bronze. — What makes this the Bronze Age if they are working copper?

20. **"the silk roads reconnected them with the western half of Eurasia"** — The silk roads are mentioned without any indication of when they connected. — When did the silk roads open?

21. **"the Wadi Hammamat trade route ran (we will hear more about Wadi Hammamat in the next chapter)"** — Forward reference that is never actually paid off. Wadi Hammamat does not appear in Chapter 4. — Unfulfilled forward reference.

## Chapter 4 — Naqada III: Writing, Kings, and the Road to Unity

22. **"a town called This (or Thinis, both spellings appear in modern English; the ancient Egyptian name was something like Tjenu, pronounced roughly 'CHEN-oo')"** — Three different names for the same town in a single sentence is confusing. — Which name should I remember?

23. **"the cult of Osiris (the god of the dead and the underworld)"** — Osiris is defined briefly here but was also briefly mentioned in Ch 1 (cattle counts paragraph references Ch 7). This is the first real explanation. Fine, but his relationship to the Abydos cemetery is asserted without explanation. — Why did Abydos become Osiris's cult center specifically?

24. **"pollen and seed evidence shows the grapes were Levantine"** — How does pollen inside a jar prove the grapes came from the Levant? — What is the method here?

25. **"pressure-flaked from a single piece of stone"** — Pressure-flaking is not defined. — What is pressure-flaking?

26. **"the niched-palace facade (a particular ornamental treatment of mudbrick walls that mimicked the niched temple platforms of Sumerian cities)"** — The word "niched" is doing a lot of work here but is never explained visually. — What does a niched facade look like? What is a niche in this context?

27. **"the cylinder seal (a small carved cylinder rolled across wet clay to leave a continuous stamped impression)"** — Good definition, but no explanation of why this technology mattered or what advantage it had over other marking methods. — Why was the cylinder seal important?

28. **"figures that scholars have nicknamed 'King Iry-Hor,' 'King Ka,' and 'Scorpion II'"** — Three names are introduced in quick succession with very little to distinguish them. — How do I keep Iry-Hor, Ka, and Scorpion II apart?

29. **"the Followers of Horus"** — The kings of the Thinite line are said to have adopted this title, but no explanation is given for what the phrase meant to contemporaries or what ritual obligations it carried. — What did being a "Follower of Horus" actually entail?

## Chapter 5 — Narmer Unifies Egypt

30. **"between two bovine heads (probably Hathor, the cow goddess) at the top of the palette"** — The Hathor heads at the top of the palette are mentioned but their function in the composition is not explained. — Why are cow heads framing the palette? What do they mean in this position?

31. **"A label from the tomb of Hor-Aha at Abydos appears to show the name 'Meni' in a context associated with Narmer's successor"** — "In a context associated with" is vague — what context? — What does the label actually say or show?

32. **"niched temple platforms of Sumerian cities"** — The niched facade is referenced again but still never described concretely enough to picture. — What does it actually look like?

33. **"the Double Crown (pschent)"** — The Double Crown is named but the reader is not told what it looks like beyond "stacks the White Crown inside the Red Crown." — How does one crown sit inside another?

34. **"Nekhbet and Wadjet (the vulture and cobra, patron goddesses of Upper and Lower Egypt respectively)"** — These goddesses appear for the first time with definitions, but the reader is told nothing about why a vulture and a cobra were chosen. — Why a vulture for the south and a cobra for the north?

35. **"the Sedge and the Bee (the heraldic plants of the two halves)"** — A bee is not a plant. — Is the bee a heraldic animal, not a plant?

## Chapter 6 — The First Dynasty: Building the Pharaonic System

36. **"damnatio memoriae (Latin for 'condemnation of memory,' the deliberate destruction of a dead person's name and image, usually by political enemies)"** — em-dash used for definition; should be parentheses per style rules. — Em-dash definition.

37. **"the nomes were probably not invented by the First Dynasty; they were more likely pre-existing tribal or chieftain territories that the unified state absorbed and standardized"** — This is asserted but not explained. How did 42 separate tribal territories become a numbered administrative system? — What was the process of standardizing 42 territories?

38. **"a military campaign into Nubia — the earliest documented Egyptian attack south of the First Cataract"** — em-dash used for what is essentially a definition/explanation. — Em-dash definition.

39. **"He conducted what his labels call the 'first time of counting' — probably the first organized census of livestock and grain"** — em-dash used for definition again. — Em-dash definition.

40. **"Byblos in turn became culturally semi-Egyptian — its kings used Egyptian titles, worshipped Egyptian gods, and wrote their names in hieroglyphs"** — When did this happen? During the First Dynasty or later? — Timeframe unclear.

41. **"exotic goods from Nubia (ebony, ivory, incense, leopard skins)"** — The parenthetical lists trade goods but never says what Egypt sent south to Nubia in return. — What did Egypt trade to Nubia?

42. **"funerary enclosures or sometimes 'valley enclosures'"** — These enclosures are described as "large rectangular walled courtyards" but their size is never given. — How large were they?

## Chapter 7 — The Second Dynasty Crisis

43. **"the Palermo Stone (a later annal fragment that records some Second Dynasty events in terse year-by-year entries)"** — The Palermo Stone is introduced parenthetically with almost no context. — When was the Palermo Stone made? What is it physically? A stone slab? A stele?

44. **"Hetepsekhemwy left it behind and was buried at Saqqara instead"** — The reader is told his tomb was probably destroyed by the Step Pyramid construction. But was the move to Saqqara his choice or was he excluded from Abydos? — Was the move to Saqqara voluntary or forced?

45. **"He was the god who stood on the bow of the solar bark and fought the chaos serpent Apophis every night"** — "Solar bark" and "Apophis" appear without definition. — What is the solar bark? Who or what is Apophis?

46. **"the Turin Canon"** — Named alongside Manetho and the Abydos king list as a source but never described. — What is the Turin Canon?

47. **"There may have been rival kings ruling simultaneously from different parts of Egypt"** — This is a significant claim made without any specific evidence cited. — What evidence suggests simultaneous rival kings?

48. **"the Longshan culture was producing wheel-made black pottery and building the first rammed-earth walls around its settlements (Ancient China Ch 2)"** — "Rammed-earth walls" is not defined. — What is rammed earth?

## Chapter 8 — Khasekhemwy and the Eve of the Pyramid Age

49. **"Nimaathap was not a ruling queen in the manner of Merneith. She was Khasekhemwy's principal wife and the mother of his heir, and her political importance is entirely biological"** — "Her political importance is entirely biological" is a strong claim that is immediately contradicted by the statement that her cult was maintained and offerings continued. Cult maintenance is not biological. — The claim and the evidence don't match.

50. **"Gisr el-Mudir enclosure at Saqqara, dating to roughly the same period, also uses crude stonework and may have been slightly earlier"** — Gisr el-Mudir is mentioned for the first time in the last chapter with no explanation of what it was for. — What was the Gisr el-Mudir? Who built it and why?

51. **"the first man-made mountain in history"** — The Step Pyramid is called "the first man-made mountain," but Mesopotamian ziggurats (temple platforms) also existed. — Were there not earlier large structures in Mesopotamia?

52. **"his 47,209 dead"** — The body-count number is used as a literary flourish in the final sentence, but the text earlier acknowledged it is "probably exaggerated." Ending the narrative on an exaggerated number without qualification undercuts the careful uncertainty shown elsewhere. — Exaggerated number presented as fact in the closing line.

53. **"the chaos serpent Apophis"** — Referenced in both Ch 7 and Ch 8 contexts but never defined beyond "chaos serpent." — What is Apophis in Egyptian mythology? Is it a real snake, a cosmic force, a demon?

**Total findings: 53**
